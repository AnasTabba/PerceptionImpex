# Community Presence Playbook (Reddit, Forums, Q&A)

> The goal is **genuine mentions**, not link spam. Real, helpful participation is
> what earns trust signals — and is exactly the kind of content LLM search
> (ChatGPT / Perplexity fan-out queries) surfaces and cites. We explicitly rejected
> the fake-"reddit-slug" parasite tactic; this is the legitimate version of the same
> insight: be genuinely present where sourcing conversations happen.

---

## The one rule that keeps this from backfiring

**Help first. Link almost never.** On Reddit especially, dropping your website into a
thread reads as spam, gets removed, and can get the account/domain shadow-banned —
the opposite of what we want. The playbook:

- Spend the first weeks **only** answering questions helpfully, no links.
- Mention the business only when it's genuinely the answer to what was asked, and
  prefer describing it in plain words over pasting a URL.
- Follow each community's self-promotion rules (most allow ~1 promo per 10 genuine
  contributions — the "9:1 rule").
- Use a real, consistent identity. Don't run multiple sockpuppets.

A single upvoted, genuinely-helpful comment where "Perception Impex" is a natural
mention is worth more than 100 dropped links.

## Where to participate

### Reddit (read each sub's rules before posting)
- **r/Textiles** — general textile/yarn discussion; sourcing and materials questions.
- **r/manufacturing** — buyers asking about suppliers and sourcing.
- **r/smallbusiness** / **r/Entrepreneur** — occasional "where do I source X" threads.
- **r/knitting** / **r/crochet** — mostly hobbyist (retail, not B2B) — low priority,
  only if a genuine bulk/sourcing question appears.
- **r/pakistan** / **r/karachi** — relevant only for local-business or trade threads.

Realistically B2B yarn sourcing has *thin* Reddit volume — that's fine. A few genuine
contributions is the target, not daily posting. Don't force it.

### Textile / trade forums & Q&A (often higher intent than Reddit for B2B)
- **Fibre2Fashion** community / forums — industry-native audience.
- **Quora** — answer questions like "How do I source cotton yarn from Pakistan?",
  "PC vs CVC yarn difference", "What's a good MOQ for yarn?" — evergreen, indexed,
  and LLM-visible.
- **LinkedIn** — post as the founder/company on yarn sourcing topics; comment on
  textile-industry posts. High trust, no spam stigma, good for B2B.
- **TradeWheel / ExportersIndia buyer-lead threads** — respond to real buying leads.

## What genuinely-helpful contributions look like

The move is to answer the *question*, demonstrate expertise, and let the credibility
imply the business. Examples:

**Q: "What's the real difference between PC and CVC yarn?"**
> PC (polyester-cotton) is usually polyester-dominant — e.g. 65/35 poly/cotton — so
> it's stronger, cheaper, and more wrinkle-resistant but less breathable. CVC ("chief
> value cotton") flips that: cotton is the majority (say 60/40 cotton/poly), so it
> feels more like cotton while keeping some poly durability. Pick PC for cost/strength,
> CVC when hand-feel and breathability matter more. Blend ratio and count (Ne) drive
> both price and end-use, so specify those when you ask a mill or trader for a quote.

*(No link. Pure value. If someone then asks "who do you buy from," that's the natural
opening to mention the business.)*

**Q: "How do I source cotton yarn from Pakistan without getting burned?"**
> A few things that save headaches: (1) always get a physical sample and confirm the
> exact count (Ne) and blend before bulk; (2) agree payment terms up front — 30/60/90
> day terms are normal for established buyers; (3) work through a trading house/dealer
> rather than cold-contacting mills if your volumes are moderate — they consolidate
> across mills and handle QC. MOQs from traders are often around a few hundred bags/
> month. Verify they've been around a while and can show client references.

*(This positions the exact model Perception Impex runs, honestly and usefully.)*

## Cadence

- **Weeks 1–4:** build the account/identity, contribute 2–3 genuine answers/week,
  **zero** promotion. Establish that you're a real practitioner.
- **Month 2+:** continue helping; mention the business only where it's the honest
  answer, ~1 in 10 contributions max. Prefer LinkedIn/Quora for anything promotional
  (they tolerate it; Reddit doesn't).
- **Ongoing:** one genuine contribution/week is plenty. Consistency > volume.

---

## What NOT to do (these get you penalized)

- ❌ Dropping `perceptionimpex.com` into unrelated threads.
- ❌ Creating a page or post with "reddit" in the slug/title to fake consensus
  (evaluated and rejected — see the chat rationale: wrong search demand for B2B yarn,
  brand risk, and the tactic is decaying since Google removed `num=100`).
- ❌ Multiple fake accounts upvoting each other.
- ❌ Incentivized or fake reviews/testimonials anywhere.
- ❌ Copy-paste identical promo text across communities (spam-filter magnet).

The whole strategy is credibility. Anything that trades short-term links for
long-term trust is a net loss for a B2B supplier whose product *is* reliability.
